History

Even the traditional strengths of U.S. medicine are dwindling. The U.S. leads the world in public funding for medical research. In 2011, the U.S. government invested $50.5 billion in medical research, accounting for 49% of public funding for medical research worldwide. But doesn’t paint the full picture. talk about decline of funding...Medical education but lack of doctors / 1000 persons...Technology pricey

Research

49% of public research funding throughout the world comes from the U.S., amounting to $50.5B annually. link

However, U.S. spending on medical research been increasing by less than the inflation rate since 2012. Not only is funding decreasing, but funding as a percentage of GDP is also decreasing. link

Education

According to US News and World Reports, the US has 8 of the top 10 medical schools in the world. link

However, the U.S. has fewer doctors per capita than most other industrialized nations. link

Technology

We have more MRI, CT, and PET machines per capita than any other country. link

However, we spend significantly more per capita than any other industrialized country. link

Diagnosis

The healthcare system in the U.S. is in dire need of a transformation. Without a doubt healthcare is complex and nuanced, but even still we lag far behind most industrialized nations. We spend xyz per capita[graph], more than any other nation. Despite this, we have worse outcomes, lower quality, and more limited access[graphs]. The Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act

(Obamacare)

access (individual mandate), no pre-existing conditions, healthcare insurance exchanges; incentivize outcomes (a little)

was a step in the right direction, but our system has a long way to go. Health is a human right, and healthcare is undeniably a public good. But government regulation and changing public perception isn’t the full answer. The patriarchal medical culture needs to change to embrace innovation and engage patients.
