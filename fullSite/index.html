Saving U.S. Healthcare

Research

The U.S. government invests $50.5 B in medical research, accounting for 49% of public research funding throughout the world. link

However, U.S. spending on medical research been increasing by less than the inflation rate since 2012. Not only is funding decreasing, but funding as a percentage of GDP is also decreasing. link

Education

According to US News and World Reports, the US has 8 of the top 10 medical schools in the world.

However, the U.S. has fewer doctors per capita than most other industrialized nations.

However, we spend significantly more per capita than any other industrialized country.
