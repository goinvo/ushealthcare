Saving U.S. Healthcare

What's Good(ish)

Research Education Technology
49% of public research funding throughout the world comes from the U.S., amounting to $50.5B annually. According to US News and World Reports, the US has 8 of the top 10 medical schools in the world. We have more MRI, CT, and PET machines per capita than any other country.
link link link
However, U.S. spending on medical research been increasing by less than the inflation rate since 2012. Not only is funding decreasing, but funding as a percentage of GDP is also decreasing. However, the U.S. has fewer doctors per capita than most other industrialized nations. However, we spend significantly more per capita than any other industrialized country.
link link link

What's Wrong

  • Cost
  • Quality
  • Access

In 2012, the U.S. spent nearly 17% of its GDP on healthcare. The UK spent less than 10%.

Despite spending far more than other industrialized nations, the U.S. lags far behind in terms of quality. link

In addition to quality, health insurance coverage in the U.S. is also abysmal, though it has improved with the Affordable Care Act. link
